Workshop for Committed Volunteer Journalists: Alain Ah-Vee on Creative Polemics

06.06.2019

Alain Ah-Vee gave an introduction at the Workshop for Committed Journalists that LALIT held on 1 June at our party headquarters on the importance of the polemic as a type of article. He said that, as well as news reporting, analysis of events, interviews of people with a particular perspective and articles that explain demands or programmatic points, there is also another important type of article for left-wing publications: creative polemics. This method has been tried and tested by revolutionary socialists, he said. It forms part of revolutionary action.


Â For example, he said, Karl Marx used this form for opposing another socialist, Proudhon, who was more idealist in his writings than rooted in material reality. Marx wrote a whole book in polemic style. See MisÃ¨re de la philosophie (The Poverty of Philosophy). Friedrich Engels used the same polemic form for his book Anti-Duhring in which he criticized Eugene Duhring. So they used polemics to criticize, in particular, those ideas that would otherwise influence the working class movement negatively, thus showing up the limitations and dangers of these ideas. The revolutionary socialists often used irony and wit in these polemics.

This method of writing was used extensively, he said, during the 1970s and 1980s, but has been used less since then. Articles, thus, describe and chronicle the daily lives of ordinary people, people who live by forced labour for a wage and who are victims of this oppression. Other articles criticize this lived reality, de-structure the reality for all to see, and dissect it, unmask it, expose it from its veils of thick propaganda, formal education, advertising manipulation.
